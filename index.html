EXTERNAL BALLISTICS CALCULATOR

Built by a passionate hunter and handloader from Sweden, primarily for practical hunting applications at ranges up to 200 m (220 yd) — rather than for precision long-range competition.

A key goal is pedagogy. The three-phase model makes the causal chain explicit: what is the rifle's mechanical zero, what does the atmosphere contribute, and what does powder temperature contribute. The sensitivity analysis visualises which parameters actually matter. The Model & Mathematics section exposes the equations openly, and the tooltips explain the physics behind each input. The aim is to build intuition, not just produce a number.

The focus is on the three-phase model: separating the rifle's zero, the external ballistic effect of weather, and (in a future version) the internal ballistic effect of powder temperature on muzzle velocity.

Trajectory validated against JBM Ballistics and QuickTARGET to <10 mm (0.4 in) at ≤200 m (220 yd). Beyond 200 m, accuracy decreases — see Model & Mathematics for details.

Trajectory Model
Point-mass ODE integrated with RK4 (dt = 1 ms):
dvx/dt = −a_drag · (vx/v)
dvy/dt = −g·cos(α) − a_drag · (vy/v)

where:
a_drag = ½ · ρ · v² · Cd(M) · k / BCSI
k = 1.55  (empirical correction factor)
BCSI = BCG1 × 703.07  [kg/m²]
ρ = 1.225 · (P/1013.25) · (288.15/T)  [kg/m³]
vs = 331.3 · √(T/273.15)  [m/s]
M = v / vs  (Mach number)

G1/G7 drag function Cd(M): standard PRODAS tabulation (22 Mach points, 0–5), with k=1.55 correction calibrated against JBM Ballistics.
Zero angle θ: found by bisection (60 iterations, ε < 10⁻¹⁰ rad).
Wind drift: lag-time method — z = w · (tflight − x/v₀).
Shooting angle α: reduces effective gravity (g·cos α). Both uphill and downhill hit higher than level.
Three-Phase Model
Phase 1 — Zero: bisection solves for θ under zero-day atmosphere (Tzero, Pzero). θ is fixed thereafter — it represents the rifle's mechanical setting.
Phase 2 — Hunt: same θ, new atmosphere (Thunt, Phunt). Shows the pure external ballistic effect of weather change. Typically <2 mm at ≤200 m.
Phase 3 — Propellant (planned): powder temperature affects v₀ via burn rate (internal ballistics). This is the dominant weather effect in practice and will be added in a future version.

Model Notes
▸ Trajectory, velocity, and energy validated to <1% vs JBM — achieved via empirical drag correction factor (1.55×) calibrated against JBM Ballistics and Norma factory data.
▸ Reliable to ≤300 m — trajectory error <2 mm, velocity error <2 m/s across the full range.
▸ G1 and G7 drag models supported — correction factor applied uniformly; G7 recommended for VLD/boattail bullets.
▸ No spin drift, Coriolis, or aerodynamic jump — negligible at ≤300 m.
▸ Phase 3 (propellant temperature) not yet implemented — this is the dominant real-world weather effect.
